## 📘 Course Overview

This repository contains all Group Work Projects (GWPs) for the **MScFE 620: Derivative Pricing** course at WorldQuant University. The course covers the theory and computational methods for pricing financial derivatives, including binomial tree models, the Black-Scholes framework, Monte Carlo simulation, and stochastic volatility models.

## 📂 Projects

### [Project 1 — Binomial Tree Option Pricing](./Project_1/)

**Topic:** Binomial tree models for pricing European and American options.

**Key Topics:**
- Binomial tree construction (up/down factors, risk-neutral probabilities)
- European Call and Put option pricing via backward induction
- American option pricing with early exercise
- Delta hedging and multi-step binomial models

---

### [Project 2 — Black-Scholes Option Pricing & Greeks](./Project_2/)

**Topic:** Analytical Black-Scholes framework and Monte Carlo simulation for European options.

**Key Topics:**
- Black-Scholes formula for European Call and Put options
- Option Greeks: Delta, Gamma, Vega, Theta, Rho
- Monte Carlo simulation with Geometric Brownian Motion
- Delta and Gamma hedging strategies

---

### [Project 3 — Heston Model & Stochastic Volatility](./Project_3/)

**Topic:** Heston stochastic volatility model for option pricing.

**Key Topics:**
- Heston model (CIR variance process)
- Monte Carlo simulation of Heston paths
- Cholesky decomposition for correlated Brownian motions
- Heston Call and Put option pricing
- Delta and Gamma under stochastic volatility
